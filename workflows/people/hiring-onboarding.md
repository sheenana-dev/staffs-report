# Hiring and Onboarding

## Purpose
Bring the right people into B-Ticket and set them up for success. A bad hire costs months of productivity. A great hire with bad onboarding still underperforms for weeks. This workflow covers both.

## When
- Hiring: When a role needs to be filled (resignation, new position, team growth)
- Onboarding: First 30 days of every new hire

## Part 1: Hiring

### Step 1: Define the Role (Before posting)
1. Meet with the department lead to clarify:
   - What will this person DO day-to-day?
   - What skills are essential vs nice-to-have?
   - What's the budget for this role?
   - Is this a replacement or a new position?
2. Write a clear job description (see template)
3. Decide on the hiring process (how many rounds, who interviews)

### Step 2: Source Candidates
For the Philippines market, prioritize these channels:
- **JobStreet** — Most widely used job platform in PH
- **LinkedIn** — Good for experienced hires
- **Facebook Groups** — PH-specific job groups are very active
- **Employee referrals** — Offer a referral bonus (even PHP 1,000-2,000 motivates)
- **University partnerships** — For junior roles (UP, Ateneo, La Salle, UST tech/business programs)
- **Kalibrr** — Growing PH job platform

### Step 3: Screen and Interview

**Round 1: Resume Screen (Department lead)**
- Does their experience match the essential requirements?
- Filter out clear mismatches
- Target: Narrow to 5-8 candidates

**Round 2: Initial Interview (Department lead, 30 min)**
- Skills assessment and culture fit
- Can they communicate clearly?
- Are they genuinely interested in B-Ticket?
- Target: Narrow to 2-3 candidates

**Round 3: Technical/Practical Assessment**
- IT: Coding challenge or take-home task (give 2-3 days)
- Design: Portfolio review + small design exercise
- Sales: Role-play a merchant pitch
- Marketing: Present a sample campaign plan for B-Ticket

**Round 4: Final Interview (You, 30 min)**
- Focus on alignment with B-Ticket's mission and values
- Ask about their career goals — will this role serve them?
- Discuss salary expectations
- Answer their questions honestly

### Step 4: Make the Offer
1. Decide within 3 business days of final interview — good candidates get snatched up fast
2. Call first, then send written offer
3. Include: role title, salary, start date, benefits, reporting structure
4. Give 3-5 days to decide
5. Prepare a rejection message for other candidates (be respectful)

## Part 2: Onboarding (First 30 Days)

### Pre-Arrival (1-2 days before start)
- [ ] Workspace/equipment ready (laptop, accounts, tools)
- [ ] Add to Staff GC on Telegram
- [ ] Share the onboarding schedule
- [ ] Assign an onboarding buddy (someone on their team, not the lead)
- [ ] Notify the team about the new hire

### Day 1: Welcome
- [ ] Personal welcome — you or the department lead greet them
- [ ] Office/team tour and introductions
- [ ] Share: company overview, mission, what B-Ticket does
- [ ] Set up all tools and accounts
- [ ] Lunch with the team or buddy
- [ ] End of day: "How was your first day? Any questions?"

### Week 1: Orient
- [ ] Department lead walks through team goals and current projects
- [ ] Review their role expectations and first-month goals
- [ ] Buddy check-in daily
- [ ] Introduce cross-department contacts they'll work with
- [ ] Complete any required admin (contracts, HR forms, payroll setup)

### Week 2: Integrate
- [ ] Start contributing to real work (small, well-defined tasks)
- [ ] Attend team meetings and standups
- [ ] Department lead check-in: "What makes sense? What's confusing?"
- [ ] You have a 15-minute check-in: "How's the first two weeks?"

### Week 3-4: Accelerate
- [ ] Take on regular workload
- [ ] Buddy support tapers off — they should feel independent
- [ ] Department lead sets clear expectations for Month 2
- [ ] You do a 30-day check-in (see template)

### 30-Day Check-in Questions
```
30-Day Check-in with [Name] — [Date]

1. How are you feeling about the role so far?
2. What's been the easiest part of joining?
3. What's been the hardest or most confusing?
4. Do you have everything you need to do your job?
5. How's your relationship with the team?
6. Is there anything about B-Ticket that surprised you?
7. What's one thing we could do better for new hires?
8. Do you feel clear on what success looks like in your role?
```

## Red Flags

### Hiring Red Flags
- Hiring takes more than 4 weeks from posting to offer — you're losing good candidates
- No structured interview process — you're making gut decisions
- Only the department lead interviews — you miss culture fit perspective
- Salary expectations are discussed too late — wastes everyone's time
- No rejection messages sent — damages B-Ticket's employer brand

### Onboarding Red Flags
- New hire has no laptop or tools on Day 1 — terrible first impression
- No one explains what the company actually does — they can't be effective
- Thrown into deep end immediately with no support — they'll sink or leave
- No check-ins in the first month — problems go unnoticed until it's too late
- The team wasn't told someone new is starting — awkward for everyone
- New hire leaves within 3 months — onboarding or hiring process failed

## Philippines Hiring Notes
- **13th month pay is mandatory** — factor it into budget
- **Probationary period** is typically 6 months under Philippine labor law
- **Notice period** for resignation is usually 30 days
- **SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG** contributions are required from Day 1
- **Contractual vs regular employment** — understand the legal implications
- **Filipino work culture values** — respect, family, community. Highlight team culture in job posts
