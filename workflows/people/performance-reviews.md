# Performance Reviews

## Purpose
Give structured feedback to department leads (and guide them to do the same for their teams). Performance reviews prevent surprises, set expectations, and create a record of growth. They're also how you identify who needs support, who's ready for more, and who might not be the right fit.

## When
- Quarterly: Lightweight check-in (15-20 minutes, based on KPIs)
- Semi-annually: Full performance review (45-60 minutes)
- Full review months: June and December
- Quarterly check-in months: March and September

## Steps

### Quarterly Check-in (15-20 minutes)

This is a quick alignment conversation, not a formal review.

1. **Prepare** (10 min before):
   - Pull their department KPIs for the quarter
   - Review your 1-on-1 notes for themes
   - Identify 1 strength and 1 area for improvement

2. **During the check-in:**
   - "Here's how your KPIs look this quarter" (share data)
   - "Here's what I've noticed going well: [specific example]"
   - "Here's one area I'd like you to focus on: [specific, actionable]"
   - "How do you feel about the quarter so far?"
   - "Do you have what you need to succeed?"

3. **After:**
   - Note the key points in their performance file
   - No formal document — just your tracking notes

### Semi-Annual Full Review (45-60 minutes)

This is the formal review. Both of you prepare.

**2 Weeks Before:**
1. Send the self-assessment template to the lead (see template)
2. Request peer feedback from 2-3 people who work closely with them
3. Start drafting your review using the template

**1 Week Before:**
1. Complete your review draft
2. Collect peer feedback
3. Prepare specific examples for every point (no vague feedback)

**During the Review:**
1. **Self-assessment discussion (15 min):**
   - Let them share their self-assessment first
   - Listen for how they perceive their own performance
   - Note where your assessment aligns or differs

2. **Your assessment (20 min):**
   - Share your review organized by category (see template)
   - For every piece of feedback, give a specific example
   - Be honest but humane — the goal is growth, not punishment
   - Start with strengths before areas for improvement

3. **Goals for next period (10 min):**
   - Agree on 2-3 development goals
   - Make them specific and measurable
   - Identify what support you'll provide

4. **Close (5 min):**
   - Ask how the review felt
   - Confirm they feel it was fair
   - Schedule the first 1-on-1 to follow up on goals

**After:**
1. Finalize the review document
2. Share a copy with the lead
3. File it for future reference
4. Follow up on development goals in 1-on-1s

## Templates

### Self-Assessment (Send to Lead)
```
Performance Self-Assessment — [Name] — [Period]

Please answer these questions honestly. There are no wrong answers.

1. What were your biggest accomplishments this period?

2. What were your biggest challenges?

3. How would you rate your performance? (1-5, with 5 being exceptional)
   - Delivering results: [1-5]
   - Leading your team: [1-5]
   - Cross-department collaboration: [1-5]
   - Communication: [1-5]
   - Growth and learning: [1-5]

4. What skills do you want to develop?

5. What support do you need from me?

6. Is there anything else you want to discuss?

Please return by [date — 1 week before review].
```

### Manager's Review Template
```
Performance Review — [Name] — [Period]

OVERALL RATING: [Exceeds / Meets / Below Expectations]

CATEGORY RATINGS:

1. Delivering Results [Exceeds / Meets / Below]
   What I observed: [specific examples]
   KPI performance: [data]

2. Leading Their Team [Exceeds / Meets / Below]
   What I observed: [how their team performs, morale, retention]

3. Cross-Department Collaboration [Exceeds / Meets / Below]
   What I observed: [how they work with other departments]

4. Communication [Exceeds / Meets / Below]
   What I observed: [clarity, frequency, transparency]

5. Growth and Learning [Exceeds / Meets / Below]
   What I observed: [new skills, improvement from last review]

STRENGTHS:
- [Strength 1 with example]
- [Strength 2 with example]

AREAS FOR IMPROVEMENT:
- [Area 1 with example and specific suggestion]
- [Area 2 with example and specific suggestion]

DEVELOPMENT GOALS FOR NEXT PERIOD:
1. [Goal] — How we'll measure it: [metric]
2. [Goal] — How we'll measure it: [metric]

SUPPORT I'LL PROVIDE:
- [What you'll do to help them succeed]

PEER FEEDBACK THEMES:
- [Summarized feedback from peers — anonymized]
```

### Telegram: Performance Review Scheduling
```
Hey [name], it's time for our semi-annual performance review.

I'll send you a self-assessment form to fill out beforehand. Our review meeting will be on [date] at [time] — please block 60 minutes.

This is a two-way conversation — come prepared with your perspective. I'm looking forward to it.
```

## Rating Guide

**Exceeds Expectations**
- Consistently surpasses KPI targets
- Proactively solves problems and improves processes
- Team is high-performing and engaged
- Other departments speak highly of working with them

**Meets Expectations**
- Hits most KPI targets
- Handles responsibilities competently
- Team is stable and productive
- Reliable and consistent

**Below Expectations**
- Misses KPI targets regularly
- Needs frequent guidance on core responsibilities
- Team issues (morale, performance, retention)
- Communication gaps

**Important:** "Below Expectations" is not a death sentence. It means "here's where you need to grow, and here's how I'll help." Only become concerned if it persists for 2+ review cycles without improvement.

## Red Flags
- Skipping reviews because "there's no time" — you're avoiding difficult conversations
- All ratings are "Meets Expectations" — you're not differentiating or being honest
- Feedback is vague ("do better") — always tie to specific examples
- Surprises in the review — if they're hearing feedback for the first time here, your 1-on-1s aren't working
- Lead gets defensive — they don't feel safe. Start with strengths, show you're invested in their growth
- No development goals — the review is just a report card with no path forward

## Tips
- Never save feedback for the review — share it in real-time during 1-on-1s
- Document specific examples throughout the period — you won't remember them at review time
- Ask for feedback on YOUR performance too — it builds trust and models growth mindset
- Keep reviews private — never discuss one person's review with another lead
- If someone is significantly underperforming, don't wait for the review — address it immediately
