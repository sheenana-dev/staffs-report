# Ready-to-Send Telegram Messages

Copy-paste these directly into Telegram. Fill in the [brackets].

---

## WEEK 1: Daily GC Engagement

### Monday — Management GC: Set the Tone
```
Good morning leads!

Starting this week, I'll be more actively following up on your daily reports. If you flag a blocker or cross-department need, I'll respond same day.

If something needs my help, tag me directly. Let's have a productive week!
```

### Daily — Staff GC: Quick Responses (pick 1-2 per day)
```
Nice work on this @[name]!
```

```
Thanks for the update @[name] — keep it up!
```

```
@[name] I see you mentioned [blocker]. Let me check with [dept/person] and get back to you.
```

```
Good progress team! Keep pushing.
```

### Daily — Management GC: Respond to Leads (pick 1 per day)
```
Thanks for the update @[lead]. I noticed [specific thing]. I'll follow up with [other dept lead] on that.
```

```
@[lead] — the [issue] you flagged, is this still a blocker? What do you need from me to unblock it?
```

```
@[lead A] @[lead B] — looks like your teams need to coordinate on [topic]. Can you two connect today and update me by end of day?
```

### Friday — Staff GC: Week Wrap-up
```
Happy Friday team!

Quick highlights from this week:
- [mention 1 win you noticed from each dept, even small ones]

Before you log off, drop a quick:
1. What did you ship this week?
2. Anything carrying over to Monday?

Have a great weekend!
```

## WEEK 2: Monday Sync Launch

### Friday (Week 1) — Management GC: Announce the Monday Sync
```
Hey leads!

Starting next Monday, we'll have a weekly 30-minute sync. Just us — you four and me.

When: Every Monday, 9:00 AM
Where: [Telegram call / Google Meet / in person]
Duration: 30 minutes sharp

Come prepared with:
1. What your team shipped last week
2. Your #1 priority for this week
3. Anything you need from another department

This is about alignment — making sure we're all rowing in the same direction. See you Monday!
```

### Monday Morning — Management GC: Share the Agenda
```
Monday Sync Agenda - March [date]

1. Quick wins from last week (2 min)
2. Department updates (5 min each):
   - IT: @[lead name]
   - Design: @[lead name]
   - Sales: @[lead name]

3. Cross-department needs (10 min)

Starting at 9 AM. See you soon!
```

```
It's not a status report — we have the sync for that. This is for things like challenges, ideas, feedback, career stuff, or anything you want to talk about privately.

Can we do Tuesdays at [time]? Let me know what works for you.
```

## WEEK 4: Monthly Review Request

### Management GC: Request Monthly Reports
```
Hey leads!

We're doing our first monthly review this [Friday/date] at 2 PM. 90 minutes.

Please send me the following by [date - 2 days before]:

1. Your top 3 accomplishments this month
2. Your biggest challenge right now
3. Your key numbers:
   - IT: uptime, bugs resolved, features shipped
   - Design: requests completed, backlog size
   - Sales: merchants signed, pipeline status, onboarding turnaround
   - Marketing: downloads, active users, campaign results
4. One person from your team you want to recognize (and why)

Keep it brief — bullets are fine. We'll discuss details in the meeting.
```

## WEEKLY REPORTS: Announce to Leads

### Management GC: Introduce Weekly Reports (Send in Week 2, after first Monday Sync)
```
Hey leads!

Starting this week, I need a weekly report from each of you every Friday by 4 PM.

I've created a Google Doc template for each department — it's mostly fill-in-the-blanks so it should take you less than 15 minutes.

Here are your templates:
- IT: [Google Doc link]
- Design: [Google Doc link]
- Sales: [Google Doc link]
- Marketing: [Google Doc link]

Make a copy, fill it in every Friday, and drop the link in this GC.

Why we're doing this: Daily GC updates are good for real-time, but I need a weekly snapshot to spot patterns, catch blockers early, and make sure cross-department needs don't fall through the cracks.

First one is due this Friday. Questions? Ask away.
```

## CLICKUP ROLLOUT: Track All Department Work

### Management GC: Announce ClickUp to Leads
```
Hey leads!

We're going to start using ClickUp to track work across all departments.

Why: Right now it's hard to see what's in progress, what's blocked, and what teams need from each other. ClickUp gives us one place to track everything — and I get a dashboard to see all 4 departments at a glance.

Your boards will be simple. Just 4 columns: To Do → In Progress → Review → Done. You drag tasks across as you work.

I'll set up everything this week and invite you first so you can explore before your team joins.

Free plan — no cost to the company.

Questions? Ask away!
```

### DM to Each Lead: Personal Walkthrough
```
Hey [name]!

I'm setting up ClickUp for all departments. I've created a Space for [Department] with these lists:

- [List 1]
- [List 2]

You'll get an invite tomorrow. Take 10 minutes to explore, create a few test tasks, and let me know if anything is confusing.

On [day], I'll invite your team. I'd like you to walk them through it — just show them how to create a task, assign it, and move it across columns.

Quick start:
1. Create a task → give it a name, assignee, due date, and priority
2. Drag it from To Do → In Progress when you start
3. Drag to Review when it needs approval
4. Drag to Done when it's finished

If you need something from another department, add a tag like "needs-IT" or "needs-design" and I'll see it on my dashboard.

That's it! Simple as that.
```

### Staff GC: Announce ClickUp to Everyone
```
Hey team!

Starting this week, we're using ClickUp to track our work across all departments. Your lead will walk you through it — it's simple.

What this means for you:
- Your tasks go into ClickUp
- Drag them across columns as you work: To Do → In Progress → Review → Done
- If you need something from another department, your lead will tag it

This doesn't replace our daily GC updates — you still post in the GC. ClickUp is for tracking WHAT we're working on. The GC is for quick communication.

Your lead will show you how it works. It takes about 5 minutes to learn.

Let's go team!
```

## USEFUL ANYTIME

### When You Need to Coordinate Two Departments
```
@[lead A] @[lead B] — [Department A] needs [what] from [Department B] for [reason].

Can you two connect today and align? Let me know if there's anything blocking this.
```

### When Something is Urgent
```
URGENT — @[lead]

[What's happening]
[Who's affected]
[What I need from you and by when]

Please update me within [timeframe].
```

### When You Want to Share a Company Win
```
Team — great news!

[What happened — new merchant, download milestone, feature launch, etc.]

This is a big deal because [why it matters for B-Ticket].

Thanks to everyone who made this happen, especially [names/teams].
```

### When You Need to Address a Problem (Management GC only)
```
Leads — I want to flag something.

I've noticed [specific observation — not accusation].

I'd like us to discuss this in our next sync and figure out a fix together. If any of you have thoughts before then, DM me.
```
