# Marketing Department Management

## Purpose
Drive user acquisition, build brand awareness, and ensure consumers actively redeem coupons on B-Ticket. The Marketing team (5 people) owns the demand side of the marketplace — without users claiming deals, merchants leave.

## When
- Daily content updates (async via Telegram)
- Weekly campaign check-in (30 minutes)
- Monthly analytics review and planning
- Quarterly brand and channel strategy

## Steps: How to Manage Marketing

### Daily (5 minutes)
1. Check the Staff GC for Marketing updates — content going live and campaign status
2. Monitor any negative mentions or PR issues flagged by the team
3. If a campaign is underperforming, ask: "What's the hypothesis for why? What should we try?"

### Weekly: Campaign Check-in
1. Review with the marketing lead:
   - What content went live this week?
   - How are active campaigns performing? (downloads, redemptions, engagement)
   - What's planned for next week?
   - Any content or assets needed from Design?
2. Keep it focused — this isn't a brainstorming session, it's a status check
3. If something is working well, ask: "Can we do more of this?"

### Monthly: Analytics Review
1. Review key numbers:
   - App downloads (new users)
   - Active users (people who opened the app)
   - Coupon redemptions (the metric that matters most)
   - Social media engagement (followers, reach, interactions)
   - Cost per acquisition (how much we spend per new user)
2. Compare to last month and to target
3. Identify top-performing channel and double down
4. Identify underperforming channel and decide: fix or cut?

### Quarterly: Channel Strategy
1. Review all marketing channels:
   - Social media (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok — which works best for PH market?)
   - Paid advertising (where are we spending? what's the ROI?)
   - Partnerships and collaborations
   - Content marketing (blog, SEO, app store optimization)
   - Offline marketing (events, flyers, local partnerships)
2. Allocate budget to highest-performing channels
3. Plan major campaigns for the quarter (holidays, festivals, launch events)

## Key Metrics to Watch
- **App downloads** — New users entering the marketplace
- **Cost per acquisition (CPA)** — How much it costs to get one new user (target: below PHP 50)
- **Coupon redemption rate** — % of claimed coupons actually used (the ultimate metric)
- **Social media engagement rate** — Likes, shares, comments relative to reach
- **Active users** — Monthly/weekly active users (are people coming back?)
- **Channel ROI** — Revenue or redemptions generated per channel per peso spent

## Red Flags
- Downloads growing but redemptions flat — we're attracting the wrong users or the app experience is poor
- High CPA with low retention — we're spending to acquire users who don't stay
- Content calendar has gaps — team is reactive instead of planned
- No A/B testing happening — we're guessing instead of learning
- All budget in one channel — too risky, diversify
- Marketing and Sales not aligned on merchant categories — we're promoting deals that don't exist
- Social media followers growing but engagement dropping — vanity metrics

## Philippines Market Notes
- **Facebook is king** — Still the most-used social platform in PH. Prioritize FB content
- **TikTok is growing fast** — Short-form video works well for food/lifestyle deals
- **Payday cycles matter** — 15th and 30th are when Filipinos spend. Time campaigns accordingly
- **Local festivals and holidays** — Sinulog, Flores de Mayo, Christmas season (starts Sept in PH!) are prime campaign periods
- **Text-heavy doesn't work** — Visuals and video outperform text posts significantly
- **Influencer partnerships** — Micro-influencers (10K-50K followers) often have better ROI than big names

## Marketing-Sales Alignment Checklist
- [ ] Marketing knows which merchant categories Sales is targeting this quarter
- [ ] Sales shares merchant success stories for Marketing content
- [ ] Both teams agree on target customer persona
- [ ] Campaign schedules consider merchant onboarding timing
- [ ] Redemption data is shared so Marketing knows which deals resonate

## What NOT to Do
- Don't spread budget across every channel — focus on 2-3 that work
- Don't ignore data to chase trends — what works in PH may differ from global trends
- Don't create content without a clear goal — "because we should post daily" isn't a strategy
- Don't skip attribution — if you can't measure it, you can't improve it
- Don't let campaigns run on autopilot — check performance mid-campaign and adjust
