# Sales Department Management

## Purpose
Grow B-Ticket's merchant base and drive promotional banner revenue. This is a small but critical department with three distinct roles that form a pipeline: Sales closes → Onboarding sets up → Account Management retains.

## Team Structure

| Role | People | Focus |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Sales** | 2 | Find and close new merchants |
| **Onboarding Ops** | 1 | Collect merchant details, input into HubSpot and B-Ticket app |
| **Account Management Ops** | 1 | Monitor redemptions monthly, optimize coupons, retain merchants |

## When
- Daily activity updates (async via Telegram)
- Weekly pipeline review (30-45 minutes)
- Monthly account health review (Account Management Ops)
- Monthly target review and forecast
- Quarterly territory/strategy planning

## The Sales-to-Retention Pipeline

```
Sales closes deal → Onboarding Ops collects details → Merchant goes live → Account Mgmt monitors monthly
     (2 people)          (1 person)                                           (1 person)
```

This pipeline means a breakdown at any stage affects everyone downstream.

## Steps: How to Manage Each Role

### Sales (2 people)

**Daily (5 minutes):**
1. Check the Staff GC and Management GC for sales wins (new sign-ups) and blockers
2. Celebrate wins publicly — "Great job closing [merchant name]!" goes a long way
3. If someone is stuck on a prospect, ask: "What's the objection? How can I help?"

**Weekly: Pipeline Review (30 min)**
1. Review the pipeline:
   - How many prospects are in each stage? (lead → contacted → demo → negotiating → closed)
   - What moved forward this week?
   - What's stuck and why?
   - What's the forecast for this month?
2. Focus on the STUCK deals — that's where you add value
3. With only 2 salespeople, each deal matters more — quality over quantity

### Onboarding Ops (1 person)

**When Sales closes a merchant, Onboarding Ops runs this workflow:**

1. **Receive handoff from Sales** — Sales tags Onboarding in Telegram with merchant name and contact
2. **Contact the merchant** to collect:
   - Business photos (storefront, menu, products)
   - Operating hours
   - Signed contract
   - Coupon/deal details (what promo they'll offer)
   - Business description and category
3. **Record everything in HubSpot** — Create or update the merchant record with all details
4. **Input merchant into the B-Ticket app** — Create the merchant listing with photos, deals, and info
5. **Verify the listing** — Check that everything displays correctly in the app
6. **Notify Account Management** — Merchant is live, ready for monthly monitoring
7. **Notify Sales** — Confirm the merchant is live (so they can tell the merchant)

**Target turnaround:** Merchant should be live within 3-5 business days of signing

### Account Management Ops (1 person)

**Monthly account review workflow:**

1. **Pull redemption data** for all active merchants
2. **Flag underperforming coupons** — Low or zero redemptions in the past month
3. **Contact underperforming merchants:**
   - Share the redemption data
   - Discuss why the current coupon might not be working (wrong deal type, unappealing offer, wrong target)
   - Suggest a different coupon/promo for next month
   - Update the listing in the app with the new coupon
4. **Update HubSpot** with notes from each merchant conversation
5. **Flag at-risk merchants** — Anyone expressing frustration or considering leaving
6. **Report to Sheena** — Monthly summary of account health (see template)

**Ongoing:**
- Respond to merchant inquiries and concerns
- Coordinate with IT if there are app-side issues affecting a merchant
- Coordinate with Marketing on featuring specific merchants in campaigns

### Monthly: Target Review (All roles)
1. Compare actual vs target:
   - New merchants signed (Sales)
   - Onboarding turnaround time (Onboarding Ops)
   - Merchant retention / churn (Account Management Ops)
   - Banner revenue
2. If behind target, diagnose:
   - Not enough leads? → Marketing problem
   - Leads but no conversions? → Sales pitch or product problem
   - Slow onboarding? → Process or capacity problem
   - High churn? → Coupon strategy or product value problem
3. Adjust next month's approach based on what you learn

### Quarterly: Strategy Planning
1. Review territory coverage — are we reaching the right areas in Metro Manila?
2. Identify high-potential merchant categories (restaurants, cafes, salons, etc.)
3. Set quarterly targets that align with company growth goals
4. Review the Sales → Onboarding → Account Mgmt pipeline for bottlenecks

### Merchant Objection Quick Responses
```
"Why should I list on B-Ticket?"
→ We drive foot traffic to your business. Our users are actively looking for deals nearby. You only pay when it works.

"I already use [competitor/Facebook]"
→ B-Ticket targets deal-seekers specifically — these are high-intent customers ready to visit. We complement your existing channels.

"It's too expensive"
→ Let's look at cost per customer acquired. If each coupon brings in a paying customer, the ROI is clear. We can start with a trial period.

"I don't have time to manage this"
→ We handle everything. You confirm the deal terms, send us photos, and we take care of the rest. It takes 10 minutes.
```

## Key Metrics to Watch

**Sales (2 people):**
- **New merchant sign-ups per month** — Primary growth metric
- **Pipeline conversion rate** — What % of leads become merchants? (target: 15-25%)
- **Time to close** — Days from first contact to signed deal
- **Activity volume** — Contacts/visits per salesperson per day

**Onboarding Ops:**
- **Onboarding turnaround time** — Days from signed deal to merchant live in app (target: 3-5 days)
- **Completeness rate** — % of merchant listings with all required info (photos, hours, deal)
- **HubSpot accuracy** — All merchant records complete and up to date

**Account Management Ops:**
- **Monthly review completion** — % of active merchants reviewed each month (target: 100%)
- **Coupon swap rate** — How many underperforming coupons were replaced
- **Merchant churn rate** — % of merchants who stop listing (target: below 5%/month)
- **Merchant satisfaction** — Are merchants responsive and positive?
- **Banner ad revenue per merchant** — Promotional banner fees

## Red Flags

**Sales:**
- Pipeline is top-heavy (lots of leads, no conversions) — pitch or product problem
- Pipeline is bottom-heavy (few leads but decent close rate) — need more leads from Marketing
- Both salespeople targeting the same area — poor territory coordination

**Onboarding Ops:**
- Onboarding takes more than 7 days — bottleneck forming
- Merchants not sending required materials — follow-up process needs tightening
- HubSpot records are incomplete — creates problems for Account Management later
- Sales closes deals faster than Onboarding can process — capacity issue

**Account Management Ops:**
- Merchants churn without warning — reviews aren't catching unhappy merchants early
- Same coupon runs for 3+ months with poor redemptions — no one is proactively swapping
- Merchant complaints go unanswered for more than 48 hours
- No communication with Marketing about which merchants to feature

## Sales-Marketing Alignment
This is the most important cross-department relationship for B-Ticket:

1. **Marketing provides leads** — Sales should give feedback on lead quality
2. **Sales provides merchant stories** — Marketing uses these for content and social proof
3. **Agree on merchant categories** — Both teams should target the same types
4. **Monthly alignment meeting** — Review: Are we attracting the right merchants AND the right users?
5. **Shared metric** — Coupon redemption rate is the ultimate measure of alignment

## What NOT to Do
- Don't set targets without data — base them on historical performance and capacity
- Don't reward only closers — the team member doing great demos but fewer closes still adds value
- Don't let sales promises drive product development — IT can't build everything a merchant asks for
- Don't ignore churn — acquiring a merchant who leaves in 2 months is wasted effort
- Don't skip pipeline reviews — they're the only way to see around corners
