# Design Department Management

## Purpose
Ensure B-Ticket's brand is consistent and professional across all touchpoints. The Design team (3 people) handles graphic design — merchant marketing materials, campaign visuals, promotional banners, app store visuals, and brand identity. Note: UI/UX sits with the IT department, not Design.

## When
- Daily check-ins (async via Telegram)
- Weekly design review sessions
- Monthly brand consistency audit

## Steps: How to Manage Design

### Daily (5 minutes)
1. Glance at the Staff GC for Design-related updates — are requests flowing smoothly?
2. Watch for bottlenecks: "Waiting on feedback" or "Need requirements" means someone upstream is blocking them
3. If a request is urgent, help the designer understand WHY it's urgent and WHEN it's needed

### Weekly: Design Review
1. The design lead presents work completed and in-progress
2. Focus your feedback on:
   - Does it match B-Ticket's brand?
   - Will merchants/users understand it immediately?
   - Is it consistent with the rest of the app?
3. Avoid "I don't like the color" feedback — instead ask "What was the reasoning behind this choice?"
4. Approve, request changes, or ask for user testing data

### Monthly: Brand Consistency Check
1. Review with the design lead:
   - Are all merchant materials using current brand guidelines?
   - Is the app visually consistent across screens?
   - Are there any outdated assets still in use?
   - How's the design system (reusable components)?

### Telegram: Design Request Format (Share with other departments)
```
When requesting design work, please include:

1. What do you need? (e.g., banner, merchant flyer, social media asset, promo visual)
2. What's the goal? (e.g., promote new feature, onboard merchant)
3. Copy/text to include
4. Deadline
5. Reference examples (if any)

Post in #design-requests. Allow 3-5 business days for non-urgent work.
```

## Key Metrics to Watch
- **Design turnaround time** — From request to delivery (target: 3-5 days for standard requests)
- **Revision rounds** — Average revisions per deliverable (target: 2 or fewer)
- **Request backlog** — How many requests are waiting? With only 3 people, growing backlog = capacity issue
- **Stakeholder satisfaction** — Are Sales/Marketing/IT happy with what they're getting?

## Red Flags
- Revision rounds consistently above 3 — requirements are unclear or feedback is contradictory
- Design team doing work without briefs — leads to wasted effort and frustration
- "Rush" requests every week — with only 3 people, this kills productivity fast
- One designer handles all the "important" work — others don't grow
- Team gets pulled into UI/UX work — that belongs to IT, redirect requests there

## Design-IT Boundary
Since UI/UX sits with IT, be clear about what Design does and doesn't own:

**Design owns:** Brand visuals, merchant materials, campaign graphics, promotional banners, app store screenshots, social media assets
**IT (UI/UX) owns:** App screen layouts, user flows, interaction design, wireframes, in-app experience

**Asset handoff to IT:**
1. Design provides brand assets (logos, colors, fonts) to IT's UI/UX person
2. Assets are organized in a shared folder with clear naming
3. Design reviews final in-app visuals for brand consistency
4. Marketing campaign assets go directly from Design to Marketing

## What NOT to Do
- Don't give vague feedback like "make it pop" — be specific about what's not working
- Don't bypass the design request process for "quick" asks — they add up
- Don't let other departments dictate design decisions — Design owns the "how it looks"
- Don't skip design QA — the final product should match the design
