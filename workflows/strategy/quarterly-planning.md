# Quarterly Planning

## Purpose
Set the direction for the next 3 months. Quarterly planning connects company goals to department priorities so everyone knows what they're working toward and why.

## When
- Last week of each quarter (March, June, September, December)
- Takes 2-3 days of focused work
- Produces a plan that guides the next 13 weeks

## Steps

### Week Before: Preparation (2-3 hours)

1. **Review last quarter's results**
   - Pull KPI actuals vs targets (see [kpis-per-department.md](../strategy/kpis-per-department.md))
   - What goals were hit? Which were missed? Why?
   - What surprised you?

2. **Gather input from department leads**
   - Ask each lead to send you:
     - Top 3 achievements last quarter
     - Top 3 priorities they want for next quarter
     - Biggest challenge they're facing
     - Any resource or budget requests
   - Give them 3 days to respond

3. **Review external factors**
   - Upcoming Philippine holidays and festivals (affects both staffing and campaigns)
   - Competitor moves (new apps, promotions)
   - Market trends (new merchant categories, user behavior changes)
   - Budget constraints

### Planning Day: Build the Plan (Half-day session)

#### Attendees: You + 4 department leads
#### Format: 3-hour meeting (with breaks)

**Hour 1: Look Back (45 min)**
1. Share last quarter's scorecard (5 min)
2. Each lead shares their 3 achievements (20 min)
3. Discuss what worked and what didn't (20 min)

**Hour 2: Look Forward (60 min)**
1. Share the company's focus for next quarter (10 min)
   - What does B-Ticket need most right now? Growth? Retention? Revenue? Stability?
2. Each lead proposes their top 3 priorities (20 min)
3. Align priorities — identify conflicts and dependencies (30 min)
   - IT building a feature that Design hasn't scoped yet?
   - Sales targeting merchants Marketing isn't promoting?

**Hour 3: Commit (45 min)**
1. Finalize 2-3 goals per department (20 min)
2. Set KPI targets for the quarter (15 min)
3. Identify cross-department projects and assign leads (10 min)

### After Planning: Document and Share (1 hour)

1. Write the quarterly plan (see template)
2. Share in the Leads Telegram group
3. Each lead shares their department's goals with their team
4. Post a company-wide summary in the All Hands group

## Philippines Calendar Considerations

**Q1 (Jan-Mar):** Post-holiday recovery. New Year motivation is high. Good for launching new initiatives. Lenten season in March/April may slow Sales.

**Q2 (Apr-Jun):** Summer season — peak for restaurants and leisure. Ramp up campaigns. School break = more app usage from younger users.

**Q3 (Jul-Sep):** BER months begin (Sept) — Filipinos start Christmas shopping early. Plan holiday campaigns in advance.

**Q4 (Oct-Dec):** Peak season. Christmas is the biggest spending period. All departments should be at full capacity. Plan for reduced staffing in late December.

## Red Flags
- Goals are vague ("improve the app" instead of "reduce load time to under 2 seconds")
- Department goals don't connect to company goals — work happens but impact doesn't
- No cross-department projects identified — departments are working in silos
- Plans are too ambitious — 3 goals per department is plenty, 7 is a recipe for failure
- Last quarter's plan was never reviewed — you're planning forward without learning backward
- Department leads weren't consulted — they won't own goals they didn't help create
