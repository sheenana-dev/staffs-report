# Monthly Review Meeting

## Purpose
Check progress against quarterly goals, review KPIs, and course-correct before small problems become big ones. This is your monthly health check across all departments.

## When
- First Friday of each month, 2:00-3:30 PM
- 90 minutes maximum
- Attendees: You + department leads

## Steps

### Before the Meeting (1-2 days before)

1. Ask each department lead to submit their monthly report (see template)
2. Pull company-level KPI data (downloads, merchants, redemptions, revenue)
3. Review your notes from last month's action items — were they completed?
4. Identify the 2-3 most important things to discuss

### During the Meeting (90 minutes)

**1. Company Scorecard (10 min)**
- Show company-level KPIs: green/yellow/red
- One sentence on overall trajectory: "We're on track / we need to adjust"

**2. Department Reports (40 min, 10 min each)**
Each lead presents:
- KPIs vs targets (with red/yellow/green status)
- Top achievement this month
- Biggest challenge or risk
- What they need from other departments

**3. Deep Dive (20 min)**
- Pick ONE topic that needs deeper discussion
- Could be: a red KPI, a cross-department conflict, a new opportunity, or a process that's broken
- Goal: Leave with a clear decision or action plan

**4. Action Items (10 min)**
- List specific actions with owners and deadlines
- Review last month's action items — mark complete or carry forward
- No more than 5 action items — if there are more, prioritize

**5. Recognition (10 min)**
- Each lead nominates someone from their team for recognition
- Share what they did and why it mattered
- This is important — people who feel appreciated perform better

### After the Meeting (30 minutes)

1. Post the summary in the Leads Telegram group
2. Follow up on action items within 48 hours
3. Share recognition publicly in the All Hands group

## Templates

### Telegram: Monthly Report Request
```
Hey leads!

Monthly review is on [date]. Please send me your report by [date - 2 days]:

1. KPIs vs targets (see your department's KPI list)
2. Top achievement this month
3. Biggest challenge or risk
4. Cross-department needs
5. Team recognition nomination (who went above and beyond?)

Keep it concise — we'll discuss details in the meeting.
```

## Red Flags
- Monthly review gets cancelled or skipped — you lose visibility and accountability
- Same issues appear month after month — problems aren't actually being solved
- Department leads come unprepared — they're not taking it seriously
- No recognition happens — missed opportunity to build culture
- Action items from last month weren't touched — follow-through is broken
- The meeting runs over 2 hours — you're going too deep, keep it at the right altitude
