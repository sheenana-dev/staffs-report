# ClickUp Guide — Marketing Department

**Space:** Marketing Department (Green)
**Folder:** B-Ticket
**Lists:** Campaigns, Content Calendar, Paid Ads, Other (Daimasu, Midori, etc.)

This is your team's workspace for tracking all marketing activities. Everything lives inside one B-Ticket folder.

---

## Your Lists

### Campaigns
For **coordinated marketing pushes** — a campaign has a specific goal, timeframe, and multiple moving parts. Each campaign is one task with subtasks.

Examples:
- March Payday Promo Campaign
- B-Ticket App Launch Push — April
- Valentine's Day Merchant Spotlight Series

**Content Calendar** — individual content pieces (posts, videos, articles, stories), whether part of a campaign or standalone.

Examples:
- TikTok video — How to redeem coupons
- IG carousel — Top 5 deals this week
- Facebook post — Merchant spotlight: ABC Restaurant

**Tip:** Use **Calendar View** for this list — it shows all content plotted on a calendar by due date, so you can see gaps and overlaps at a glance.

**Paid Ads** — any marketing where you're spending money (Facebook Ads, Google Ads, Instagram boost, etc.). Tracked separately because it involves budget.

Examples:
- Facebook Ads — App install campaign (PHP 5,000/week)
- Instagram boost — March payday promo post
- Google Ads — "coupon app Philippines" search campaign

**How the 3 B-Ticket Lists Connect:**

```
Campaigns ──── uses content from ──── Content Calendar
    │                                       │
    └──── uses paid promotion from ──── Paid Ads
```

A campaign like "March Payday Promo" might have:
- 5 social posts in **Content Calendar**
- A Facebook ad in **Paid Ads**
- The campaign task itself in **Campaigns** (with subtasks linking to these)

But content and ads can also be standalone — not every IG post is part of a campaign.

### Other (Daimasu, Midori, etc.)
For **all marketing work for the company's other businesses** — Daimasu, Midori, and any non-B-Ticket brands.

Examples:
- Daimasu Instagram content — March schedule
- Midori grand opening campaign
- Daimasu Facebook ad — new menu items (PHP 3,000)

## How to Create a Task

Every task must have these fields:

| Field | Required | How to Fill It |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| **Task name** | Yes | Be specific: "TikTok video — How to redeem coupons" not just "TikTok video" |
| **Assignee** | Yes | Who's creating this content / managing this campaign / running this ad |
| **Priority** | Yes | Urgent / High / Normal / Low |
| **Due date** | Yes | Publish date (content), campaign end date, or ad launch date |
| **Description** | Yes | Brief, target audience, platforms, goals, budget (for ads) |
| **List** | Yes | Campaigns, Content Calendar, Paid Ads, or Other (Daimasu, Midori, etc.) |

### For Campaigns — Use Subtasks:
```
Task: March Payday Promo Campaign
  ├── Write campaign brief
  ├── Create social posts (5 pieces) — needs-design
  ├── Set up Facebook ad — PHP 3,000 budget
  ├── Build landing page — needs-IT
  ├── Schedule push notification — needs-IT
  └── Post-campaign report
```

### For Content Calendar — Also Include:
- **Platform**: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, Blog
- **Content type**: Post, story, reel, video, carousel, article
- **Publish date**: The exact date it goes live (this is what shows on Calendar View)

### For Paid Ads — Also Include:
- **Platform**: Facebook Ads, Google Ads, Instagram, TikTok Ads
- **Budget**: Total budget and daily/weekly spend
- **Goal**: App installs, clicks, impressions, leads
- **Run dates**: Start and end date

## Statuses — What They Mean for Marketing

| Status | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **TO DO** | Planned but not started — brief not written, content not created, ad not set up |
| **IN PROGRESS** | Actively working — writing copy, creating visuals, setting up ad campaign |
| **REVIEW** | Content/ad ready for approval before publishing — Sheena or lead reviews |
| **COMPLETE** | Published, live, or campaign ended. For ads: campaign wrapped up with results logged |

## Cross-Department Tags

Use these tags when your task needs something from another department:

| Tag | When to Use | Example |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| `needs-design` | You need visuals, banners, or creative assets | "Need 5 social media assets for March Payday Promo campaign" |
| `needs-IT` | You need a landing page, push notification, app banner, or technical integration | "Need push notification sent to all users on March 15 for promo launch" |
| `needs-sales` | You need merchant info or coordination for merchant-focused content | "Need list of 5 merchants with best deals for 'Top Deals' content series" |
| `blocked` | Task is stuck | Waiting for design assets, approval delayed, budget not confirmed |

When you add a tag, Sheena sees it on her dashboard. She'll help coordinate.

## Using Calendar View

For the **Content Calendar** list, switch to Calendar View:

1. Open your Marketing Space → Content Calendar list
2. Click **+ View** at the top → Select **Calendar**
3. You'll see all content tasks plotted by their due date

This helps you:
- Spot days with no content planned (gaps)
- See if too much content is stacked on one day
- Plan content 2-4 weeks ahead
- Quickly drag tasks to reschedule

## Your Daily Routine (5 minutes)

1. **Open your Marketing Space** → Board View
2. **Check Content Calendar** — anything publishing today or tomorrow? Is it ready?
3. **Check Campaigns** — any campaign subtasks due this week? Are they on track?
4. **Check Paid Ads** — any active ads? Quick check on spend vs. budget
5. **Update statuses** — move tasks as work progresses
6. **Tag cross-department needs** — if you need design assets or IT help, tag it now so Sheena can coordinate

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Putting everything in Campaigns** — standalone content (like a weekly IG post) goes in Content Calendar, not Campaigns. Only use Campaigns for coordinated, multi-channel pushes
- **Not tracking ad spend** — every peso spent should be in a Paid Ads task with budget and results. This feeds directly into your weekly report
- **No publish dates on content** — the Calendar View only works if every content task has a due date set to its publish date
- **Campaign tasks without subtasks** — a campaign is multiple activities. Break it down so you can track each piece separately
- **Mixing lists** — B-Ticket campaigns go in Campaigns, content in Content Calendar, ads in Paid Ads, and Daimasu/Midori work in the Other list. Don't mix them
