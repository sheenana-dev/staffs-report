# Weekly Sync Meeting

## Purpose
Align all departments on what happened last week, what's planned this week, and where cross-department coordination is needed. This is the ONE meeting where everyone gets on the same page.

## When
- Every Monday, 9:00 AM
- 30-45 minutes maximum (set a timer)
- Attendees: You + department leads (4 people)
- Via: Telegram voice/video call, Google Meet, or Zoom

## Steps

### Before the Meeting (Sunday evening or Monday 8:30 AM)
1. Review last week's goals — which were completed?
2. Check the Staff GC and Management GC for recurring themes from the past week
3. Prepare your agenda using the template below
4. Share the agenda in the Management GC 15 minutes before the meeting

### During the Meeting (30-45 minutes)
1. **Opening (2 min):** Quick win or positive note to set the tone
2. **Round-robin updates (20 min, 5 min each):**
   Each department lead answers:
   - What shipped/completed last week?
   - What's the #1 priority this week?
   - Any blocker that needs another department's help?
3. **Cross-department items (10 min):**
   - Address any dependencies flagged in the round-robin
   - Assign owners and deadlines for cross-department actions
4. **Your announcements (5 min):**
   - Company updates, changes, new priorities
   - Reinforce the quarterly goal connection
5. **Close (2 min):**
   - Recap action items
   - Confirm next week's meeting time

### After the Meeting (10 minutes)
1. Post the summary in the Management GC within 1 hour
2. Follow up on any action items assigned to you
3. Check in mid-week (Wednesday) on cross-department items

## Templates

### Telegram: Agenda (Share before meeting)
```
Monday Sync Agenda - [Date]

1. Quick wins from last week
2. Department updates (5 min each):
   - IT: [lead name]
   - Design: [lead name]
   - Sales: [lead name]
   - Marketing: [lead name]
3. Cross-department items
4. Announcements
5. Action items

Please come prepared with your top 3 from last week and #1 priority for this week.
```

```
1. [Action item] — @[owner] status?
2. [Action item] — @[owner] status?

Reply with: Done / On track / Need help
```

## Red Flags
- Meeting regularly exceeds 45 minutes — you're going too deep on topics that should be separate conversations
- One department lead dominates the conversation — actively manage airtime
- Same cross-department blocker appears 3 weeks in a row — it's not being resolved, needs escalation
- Department leads send someone else instead of attending — they're not prioritizing alignment
- No action items come out of the meeting — it was informational, not actionable
- People check their phones during the meeting — keep it short and engaging enough that they don't

## Tips
- Start on time even if someone is late — they'll learn to be on time
- Don't solve problems in this meeting — identify them, assign an owner, solve them offline
- Rotate who goes first in the round-robin — prevents last-position fatigue
- If a topic needs more than 3 minutes of discussion, "parking lot" it for a separate conversation
- End every meeting with: "What's the ONE thing we need to get right this week?"
