# Daily Standup Process

## Purpose
Keep every department moving forward and surface blockers before they become problems. This is NOT a status report — it's a quick pulse check that tells you where to focus your attention today.

## When
- Every workday, morning (between 9:00-10:00 AM)
- Async via Telegram (preferred) or 15-minute call if issues need discussion
- Skip on Philippine holidays — post a "Happy [holiday]!" instead

## How It Works: Two Group Chats

B-Ticket uses two Telegram GCs for daily reporting:

| GC | Who Reports | What They Report |
|----|-------------|-----------------|
| **Staff GC** | All regular staff | Daily work updates — what they're working on, blockers |
| **Management GC** | Department leads | Daily lead reports — department status, cross-department needs |

This means you get TWO views:
- **Staff GC** = ground-level detail (individual tasks, individual blockers)
- **Management GC** = department-level summary (priorities, risks, coordination)

## Steps

### Your Daily Routine (10-15 minutes)

**1. Scan the Staff GC (5 min)**
- Skim staff updates for patterns — who's blocked, who's quiet, what's moving
- You don't need to respond to every update — leads handle their own teams
- React with a thumbs-up or quick reply when you see good work
- If you spot a cross-department blocker, tag the relevant lead

**2. Read the Management GC (5 min)**
- Department leads post their updates here
- Focus on: What's on track? What needs your help? Any cross-department conflicts?
- If a lead raises a blocker that involves another department, coordinate directly

**3. Act on blockers (5 min if needed)**
- If you spotted anything in either GC that needs your involvement, handle it now
- If nothing needs attention, great — move on with your day

### When Things Are On Fire (Sync option)
- Pull the relevant leads into a quick 15-min call
- Keep it focused: What's broken? Who fixes it? By when?
- Post the outcome in the Management GC

## Templates

### Management GC: When You Spot Something in Staff GC
```
Heads up @[lead] — I noticed [staff member] mentioned [issue] in the staff GC.

Is this on your radar? Do you need any help with it?
```

### Management GC: Cross-Department Coordination
```
@[lead A] @[lead B] — Looks like [department] is waiting on [department] for [what].

Can you two sort this out today? Let me know if there's a blocker I need to remove.
```

### Staff GC: Friday Wrap-up
```
Happy Friday team!

Quick wrap-up:
1. What did you ship this week?
2. Anything carrying over to next week?
3. Shoutout a teammate who helped you this week!

Have a good weekend!
```

### Staff GC: When You Want to Celebrate a Win
```
Shoutout to @[person] for [specific accomplishment]!

This matters because [why it's impactful for B-Ticket].

Great work!
```

## Red Flags
- Someone stops posting in Staff GC for 3+ days — check in privately, not in the group
- Staff GC becomes a complaint thread — redirect issues to the relevant lead
- Leads stop posting in Management GC — they're not prioritizing communication with you
- Same blocker appearing for 3+ days in either GC — it's not being resolved, escalate
- Staff GC gets noisy with non-work chat — establish ground rules or create a separate social GC
- Management GC is silent — leads should be reporting daily even if it's just "all on track"

## Tips for New Managers
- Don't treat GC updates as surveillance — it's a support tool, not a tracking tool
- Respond to blockers with action, not questions — "I'll talk to Design about this" beats "Why is this blocked?"
- Celebrate completed work — a simple "Nice work!" in the Staff GC boosts morale
- Let leads handle their own team's updates — don't micro-manage individual staff through the Staff GC
- Your own updates: Consider posting what YOU are working on in the Management GC — it builds trust and transparency
